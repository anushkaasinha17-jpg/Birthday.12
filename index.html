A Letter I Mean Every Word Of

My dearest Mayank,

I want to start by telling you something simple: knowing you has made my life better. Not in a dramatic, cinematic way — in the quiet, daily way that matters more. In the way you remember the small things I mention once. In the way you show up, again and again, even on the days it would be easier not to. That kind of love is rare, and I don't take it for granted.

There's a particular kind of luck in finding someone whose presence makes ordinary days feel worth looking forward to. A random Tuesday becomes something good because I know I'll talk to you. A hard week becomes bearable because I know you're on the other end of it, rooting for me. I used to think love was supposed to be loud and dramatic. You taught me it's mostly steady — mostly showing up, mostly choosing each other on the boring days too. I'm endlessly grateful for that steadiness.

I am proud of you in ways I don't say often enough. I watch you work through hard things — the doubts, the pressure, the days when nothing seems to go right — and I see someone who keeps getting up anyway. That's not a small thing. That's character. Whatever you're building, whatever you're chasing, I hope you know I see the effort behind it, even when the results are slow to arrive. You don't have to prove anything to me. I'm already proud of who you are becoming, one imperfect day at a time.

I think about our future sometimes, in the unglamorous, specific way — not just grand trips or big milestones, but quiet mornings, inside jokes only we'll understand, the home we might build together someday, the version of us that's had more time to grow. I want to be there for the version of you that hasn't happened yet — the one with more confidence, more peace, more of the success you're working so hard for. I want a front row seat to that. Whatever shape our dreams take, individually or together, I hope we keep choosing to walk toward them side by side.

I also want you to know this: you don't have to be endlessly strong around me. You're allowed to be tired. You're allowed to not have it figured out. I'm not here for the highlight reel — I'm here for all of it, the good days and the heavy ones. If the world ever feels like too much, I hope you remember there is one person, at least, who is completely, unconditionally in your corner. That's me. That's not going to change.

So today, on your birthday, I hope you feel how much you're loved — not just by me, but for exactly who you are. Not the version people expect, not the version that has it all together — just you, as you actually are. I love your humor, your mind, the way you care about things quietly instead of announcing it. I love that being loved by you feels safe. I hope this year hands you more of what you deserve: peace when you need rest, momentum when you're ready to chase something, and people around you who see your worth as clearly as I do.

Thank you for being you. Thank you for choosing me, on ordinary days and hard ones. Whatever comes next, I'll be here, cheering loudly, quietly proud, endlessly grateful that out of everyone, it's you.

Always yours,
Me
